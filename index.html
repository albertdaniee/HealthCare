The Four HIPAA Rules

HIPAA's regulatory framework is divided into four distinct rules, each governing a specific dimension of healthcare data handling.

🔒

Privacy Rule

Establishes national standards for the protection of individuals' medical records and other personal health information. Governs how covered entities use and disclose PHI and grants patients rights over their information.

45 CFR Part 164
🛡️

Security Rule

Sets standards for protecting electronic PHI (ePHI) through administrative, physical, and technical safeguards. Requires covered entities to assess risks and implement appropriate security measures.

45 CFR §164.302–318
🔔

Breach Notification Rule

Requires covered entities to notify affected individuals, HHS, and in some cases the media, within 60 days of discovering a breach of unsecured PHI. Business associates must notify covered entities within 60 days.

45 CFR §164.400–414
⚡

Enforcement Rule

Establishes HIPAA violation investigation and penalty procedures. OCR can impose civil monetary penalties ranging from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with an annual cap of $1.9 million per violation category.

45 CFR Part 160
📋

Transaction & Code Set Rule

Mandates the use of specific EDI transaction sets and code sets for electronic healthcare transactions. Covers claims, remittances, eligibility inquiries, referrals, and more using ANSI X12 standards.

45 CFR Part 162
🔢

Identifier Standards

Requires covered entities to use standardized identifiers: the National Provider Identifier (NPI) for providers, Employer Identification Number (EIN) for employers, and the National Health Plan Identifier (HPID) for health plans.

45 CFR Part 162 Sub E–N

HIPAA EDI Transaction Sets

HIPAA mandates ANSI ASC X12 electronic data interchange standards for all covered healthcare transactions between trading partners.

837-P

Professional Claim

Physician and outpatient claims submitted to payers. Replaces the paper CMS-1500 form.

837-I

Institutional Claim

Hospital and facility claims submitted to payers. Replaces the paper UB-04 form.

835

Payment & Remittance

Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA) from payer to provider detailing claim adjudication.

270/271

Eligibility Inquiry / Response

Verify patient benefits and insurance coverage before providing services.

276/277

Claim Status Inquiry / Response

Check on claim status after submission. Allows providers to track claim progress.

278

Authorization & Referral

Request and receive prior authorization or referral for healthcare services.

820

Premium Payment

Employers transmit premium payments to health plans on behalf of enrollees.

834

Benefits Enrollment

Transmit enrollment and maintenance of employee benefit plan information to insurers.

999

Implementation Acknowledgment

Confirms syntactical acceptance or rejection of an EDI interchange by the receiver.

Compliance Roadmap

A structured approach to achieving and maintaining HIPAA compliance for covered entities and business associates.

01

Risk Assessment

Conduct a comprehensive analysis of potential risks and vulnerabilities to ePHI confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Document all findings and the likelihood and impact of identified risks.

02

Policies & Procedures

Develop and implement written HIPAA policies covering access controls, workforce training, incident response, and breach notification. Policies must be reviewed and updated periodically.

03

Business Associate Agreements

Execute BAAs with all vendors, contractors, and subcontractors who create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on your behalf. BAAs must include specific regulatory provisions per 45 CFR §164.504(e).

04

Technical Safeguards

Implement access controls, audit controls, integrity controls, and transmission security for all systems that handle ePHI. Encryption at rest and in transit is strongly recommended (addressable standard).

05

Workforce Training

Train all workforce members who handle PHI on HIPAA requirements, your organization's policies, and their specific roles and responsibilities. Training must be documented and refreshed periodically.

06

Ongoing Monitoring & Audit

Implement audit logging for all ePHI access and activity. Conduct periodic internal audits, review access logs, and perform gap assessments to ensure continued compliance and identify new risks.

Civil Monetary Penalties

OCR enforces HIPAA violations across four penalty tiers based on the level of culpability. Criminal penalties are separate and can include imprisonment.

Tier 1 — No Knowledge
$100–$50K

The entity did not know and could not have known of the violation. Annual cap: $25,000.

Tier 2 — Reasonable Cause
$1K–$50K

Violation due to reasonable cause and not willful neglect. Annual cap: $100,000.

Tier 3 — Willful Neglect Corrected
$10K–$50K

Willful neglect but violation was corrected within 30 days. Annual cap: $250,000.

Tier 4 — Willful Neglect Uncorrected
$50K+

Willful neglect with no correction. Annual cap: $1.9 million. Criminal referral possible.
